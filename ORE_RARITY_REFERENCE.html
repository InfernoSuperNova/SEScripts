Key Insights

üî• Fusion Reactor Dominates

The Prototech Fusion Reactor is the clear winner for power generation, yielding 11.11 kWh per ice voxel. It consumes hydrogen at 1000 L/s and outputs 400 MW, making it 40x more efficient than the standard Hydrogen Engine.

‚ö° Nickel Battery Cycle is Competitive

The nickel battery cycle yields ~6.8 kWh per voxel, making it surprisingly competitive with uranium. However, it requires significant resource investment (133 nickel voxels to build a single large battery).

‚ò¢Ô∏è Uranium is Inefficient

Despite being a "power ore," uranium yields only 3 kWh per voxel due to its extremely low mining yield (0.3 ore per voxel) and low refining efficiency (0.01 ingot per ore).

üí® Hydrogen Engine is Wasteful

The standard Hydrogen Engine is the least efficient option at 0.2778 kWh per ice voxel. While it uses the same fuel as the Fusion Reactor, its low power output (5 MW) and slow consumption rate (500 L/s) make it impractical for serious power generation.
